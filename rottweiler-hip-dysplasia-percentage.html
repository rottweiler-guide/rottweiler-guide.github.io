What is the Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage? The hard numbers

According to the Orthopedic Foundation for Animals (OFA), the Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage in tested populations is around 23%. However, this only includes dogs that have been voluntarily screened – which means the true prevalence may be higher. A 2021 study published in American Veterinary Medical Association journals suggested that when including unscreened dogs, the percentage could reach 30% or more.

This discrepancy is a justice issue: only the most conscientious breeders screen, leaving a hidden population of Rottweilers suffering without accountability. The Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage isn’t equal across the board – it varies wildly by region, breeder, and bloodline.

“The real injustice is that many Rottweilers are never even evaluated – their pain is invisible to the registry.” – Dr. Elena Vasquez, veterinary orthopaedist

Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage history: from hidden to headline

The Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage history mirrors the struggle for fairness in dog breeding. In the 1970s, the percentage was estimated at 35–40% – a direct result of overbreeding and no standardised screening. The first OFA evaluations in the 1980s brought hope, and by the 1990s the percentage had dropped to 25% thanks to breeder cooperation. But in recent years, the Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage history shows a plateau – and even a slight uptick in some registries. Why? Because voluntary testing is not enforced, and back‑yard breeders avoid the system.

This history is a call to action. We need mandatory transparency – not charity. The Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage should be public, per‑litter, so buyers can make informed choices. That’s true fairness.

Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage 2026: will the trend be fair?

Projections for Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage 2026 depend on which path the breed community chooses. If we continue with voluntary screening, the percentage will likely hover between 22% and 28%. But if the demand for equal treatment grows – if buyers insist on OFA or PennHIP scores – the Rottweiler hip dysplasia percentage 2026 could drop below 20%. Several European registries have already achieved this by making screening mandatory for breeding stock. Justice is possible, but only if we level the playing field.
